Gospel Salvation Guide

Jesus Christ

Jesus Christ was supernaturally begotten by the Holy Spirit, born of a virgin, and is truly, totally God and truly, totally man...

Jesus Christ was supernaturally begotten by the Holy Spirit, born of a virgin, and is truly, totally God and truly, totally man.

~Matthew 1:20-21 KJV   ~Luke 1:35 KJV

He is the sinless, omnipotent, omniscient, Saviour of sinners, King of kings, and Lord of lords.

~John 1:14 KJV   ~1 John 4:9 KJV   ~Revelation 19:16 KJV

Only through his death, burial & resurrection are sins forgiven & reconciliation with God available.

The Trinity

God has revealed his existence as, one God, eternally existing in three forms: the Father, the Son (Jesus Christ), and the Holy Spirit. All together known as the Trinity. They are co-eternal in being, co-identical in nature, co-equal in power and glory, and have the same attributes and perfections.

~Deuteronomy 6:4 KJV   ~Matthew 28:19 KJV   ~2 Corinthians 13:14 KJV   ~1 John 5:7 KJV

The Holy Scriptures

The Masoteric text (Old Testament) and the Textus Receptus (New Testament) are both preserved for us today in the King James Bible (KJV). The KJV is the inspired, inerrant, infallible, and altogether authentic, accurate and authoritative Word of God. It is therefore the supreme and final authority in all things.

~ 2 Timothy 3:15-17 KJV   ~2 Peter 1:21 KJV

The Gospel

The "good news" is the Lord Jesus Christ died on the cross for our sins; He was buried; And He arose alive and triumphant from the grave...

The "good news" is the Lord Jesus Christ died on the cross for our sins; He was buried; And He arose alive and triumphant from the grave. Jesus took the punishment we deserve and in exchange offers the free gift of salvation we don't deserve. By his grace we get heaven and by his mercy we don't get hell. All that place their full faith and trust in Jesus' sacrifice, and not their own behavior, are saved by His shed blood.

~Acts 4:12 KJV   ~Acts 16:30-31 KJV   ~Romans 5:8-9 KJV   ~1 Corinthians 15:1-4 KJV   ~Colossians 1:14 KJV   ~Ephesians 1:7 KJV   ~1 John 2:2 KJV   ~John 3:16 KJV   ~John 6:47 KJV   ~1 Peter 2:24 KJV

Baptism

Each person claiming Jesus Christ as Saviour, should promptly make public his/her profession of Christ...

Each person claiming Jesus Christ as Saviour, should promptly make public his/her profession of Christ. Every new Christian should follow the Lord's command for a believer's baptism. A scriptural baptism is always by immersion after salvation. Baptism does not save, but is the Lord's prescribed way for us to identify ourselves with Him in open confession. Baptism is a symbolic representation of the death, burial and resurrection of Christ.

~Matthew 28:18-20 KJV   ~Acts 8:36-38 KJV   ~Acts 16:30-34 KJV   ~Romans 10:9-10 KJV

The Holy Spirit

At the precise time of the new birth (salvation), the believer immediately receives the Holy Spirit and is placed into the body of Christ...

At the precise time of the new birth (salvation), the believer immediately receives the Holy Spirit and is placed into the body of Christ. The believer is forever indwelt by the Holy Spirit as Comforter, Teacher and Guide. The Holy Spirit will guide the believer into all truth.

~John 1:32-34 KJV   ~John 14:16-17 KJV   ~John 16:13 KJV   ~Acts 1:8 KJV   ~Acts 4:31 KJV   ~Acts 15:7-8 KJV   ~1 Corinthians 6:19-20 KJV   ~Ephesians 1:13 KJV

Eternal Security

Every believer, at the exact moment he/she puts their full trust in Christ, is born into the family of God...

Every believer, at the exact moment he/she puts their full trust in Christ, is born into the family of God. The believer instantaneously becomes a son or daughter of the living God. The believer has been redeemed by the Lord and is forever secure in Christ, possessing everlasting, eternal life and is sealed by the Holy Spirit for eternity. Salvation can never be lost.

~John 5:24 KJV   ~John 10:27-29 KJV   ~Romans 8:38-39 KJV   ~Ephesians 1:13 KJV

The Church

The Scriptures teach that a local church is a congregation of believers (as few as 2), associated by a covenant of faith and fellowship of the Gospel...

The Scriptures teach that a local church is a congregation of believers (as few as 2), associated by a covenant of faith and fellowship of the Gospel. The church observes the ordinances of Christ and is governed by His laws. The only scriptural officers are bishops or pastors (pastor, bishop and elder are three terms for the same office) and deacons, whose qualifications, claims and duties are clearly defined in the New Testament.

~1 Timothy 3:1-16 KJV   ~Titus 1:1-16 KJV

The true mission of the church is the faithful witnessing of Christ to all, as well as, the building up and edifying of the saints. The local church has the absolute right of self-government free from the interference of any hierarchy of individuals or organizations. The one and only Head is Christ through the Holy Spirit.

~Matthew 18:20 KJV   ~Matthew 28:20 KJV   ~John 14:15; 15:10 KJV   ~Acts 2:41-42, 47; 8:1; 14:23; 15:22 KJV   ~Romans 16:16-20 KJV   ~1 Corinthians 4:17; 11:2 KJV

Soul Winning

The Lord's Great Commission (Matthew 28:19-20 KJV) is His command to us to spread the Gospel to the world. The goal is to win un-saved people to Christ, baptize them, and teach them everything the Lord has commanded. The believer is to study the Word of God.

~Proverbs 11:30 KJV   ~Matthew 24:15 KJV   ~Matthew 28:18-20 KJV   ~Mark 16:15 KJV   ~Acts 1:8 KJV

After the Millennium, the believer will dwell with God the Father and the Lord Jesus Christ in the New Heaven and New Earth.

Christmas

Christmas is not Biblical. It was not instituted by Jesus Christ, the Apostles or any Bible authority...

Christmas is not Biblical. It was not instituted by Jesus Christ, the Apostles or any Bible authority. Jesus was not born on December the 25th. The Bible links Jesus' birth timeline to a season warm enough for shepherds to be in the field with their flocks.

Luke 2:8 KJV "And there were in the same country shepherds abiding in the field, keeping watch over their flock by night."

The Christmas festival was ordered to be celebrated by the 5th Century Roman Catholic church on the day of the Roman feast of Sol (the sun god). The Christmas festival became intertwined with the Pagan festivals of Saturnalia & Brumalia.

The Christmas tree is a celebration of the birth of Nimrod (ruler of Babylon). The custom of cutting a tree and decorating it was a heathen tradition which the Lord commanded in the Bible never to do.

Jeremiah 10:2-4 KJV "Thus saith the Lord, Learn not the way of the heathen...For the customs of the people are vain: for one cutteth a tree out of the forest...They deck it with silver and with gold; they fasten it with nails and with hammers, that it move not."

Santa Claus is pagan. He is an omnipresent, mythical character, that will only give gifts as a reward for good behavior — the antithesis of the Gospel.

Ephesians 2:8-9 KJV "For by grace are ye saved through faith; and that not of yourselves: it is the gift of God: Not of works, lest any man should boast."

Colossians 2:8 KJV "Beware lest any man spoil you through philosophy and vain deceit, after the tradition of men, after the rudiments of the world, and not after Christ."

Easter

Easter is not Biblical. It was not instituted by Jesus Christ, the Apostles or any Bible authority...

Easter is not Biblical. It was not instituted by Jesus Christ, the Apostles or any Bible authority. Easter is the Roman spring fertility festival known as Eostre. This pagan festival celebrated the goddess Ishtar (Queen of heaven) and included the dying of eggs and the baking of cakes (hot cross buns).

Jeremiah 7:18 KJV "The children gather wood, and the fathers kindle the fire, and the women knead their dough, to make cakes to the queen of heaven, and to pour out drink offerings unto other gods, that they may provoke me to anger."

The Bible records the Romans celebrating Easter, not Christians. The Apostle Peter was put in jail by the Romans for spreading the Gospel of Jesus Christ. He later escaped while the Romans were busy celebrating their pagan festival of Easter.

Colossians 2:8 KJV "Beware lest any man spoil you through philosophy and vain deceit, after the tradition of men, after the rudiments of the world, and not after Christ."
